neighbours and old acquaintances had continued between me and mrs read's family who all had a regard for me from the time of my first lodging in their house i was often invited there and consulted in their affairs wherein i sometimes was of service i piti'd poor miss read's unfortunate situation who was generally dejected seldom chearful and avoided company i considered my giddiness and inconstancy when in london as in a great degree the cause of her unhappiness tho the mother was good enough to think the fault more her own than mine as she had prevented our marrying before
