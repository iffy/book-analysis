very sincerely and with unalterable affection b franklin a letter to samuel mather passy may revd sir it is now more than years since i left boston but i remember well both your father and grandfather having heard them both in the pulpit and seen them in their houses the last time i saw your father was in the beginning of when i visited him after my first trip to pennsylvania he received me in his library and on my taking leave showed me a shorter way out of the house through a narrow passage which was crossed by a beam
