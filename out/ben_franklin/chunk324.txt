arriving at moral perfection i wish'd to live without committing any fault at any time i would conquer all that either natural inclination custom or company might lead me into as i knew or thought i knew what was right and wrong i did not see why i might not always do the one and avoid the other but i soon found i had undertaken a task of more difficulty than i had imagined while my care was employ'd in guarding against one fault i was often surprised by another habit took the advantage of inattention inclination was sometimes too strong
