too was to be made a pressman george webb an oxford scholar whose time for four years he had likewise bought intending him for a compositor of whom more presently and david harry a country boy whom he had taken apprentice i soon perceivd that the intention of engaging me at wages so much higher than he had been usd to give was to have these raw cheap hands formd thro me and as soon as i had instructed them then they being all articled to him he should be able to do without me i went on however very chearfully put
